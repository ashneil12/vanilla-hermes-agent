## How to Write Prompts That Think, Build, and Sell Like an Operator

## ✅ WHY MOST PROMPTS FAIL

Most people write prompts like this:

- “Write me a VSL for coaches.”
- “Make me an offer.”
- “Write sales copy that converts.”

That’s like telling a chef: “Make me food.”

→ What kind of food?

→ Who’s eating it?

→ Is it keto, vegan, bulk, gourmet?

→ Is it fast or fine dining?

→ Do you have ingredients? Allergies?

That’s how 95% of people prompt AI. And they wonder why the food comes out bland.

**Garbage in = garbage out.**

**Good prompt = helpful output.**

**Great prompt = scalable strategy execution.**

## ✅ THE 12 LAWS OF PROMPTCREATOR™

| Law | What It Means |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Define the Role | Tell the AI who it is (e.g. “You are a belief-based sales strategist”) |
| 2. Set the Mission | What is the specific outcome? (e.g. “Write a sales page that reframes objections”) |
| 3. Insert Rules & Logic | How should it think? What should it not do? What’s the tone? |
| 4. Ask What You’d Ask a Person | Don’t jump into “write this”, first ask for what it needs to know |
| 5. Feed Beliefs, Not Just Data | Teach it how you think, not just what you want |
| 6. Build Modularly | Split big goals into stackable mini-prompts |
| 7. Close the Loop | Ask for reflection, clarification, or refinements, don’t just accept the first result |
| 8. Show Your Work | Strategic transparency - “Think step-by-step, show reasoning” |
| 9. Match Reasoning to Complexity | Use appropriate cognitive framework (Simple=CoT, Complex=ToT, Multi-stakeholder=GoT) |
| 10. Build Uncertainty Handling | Include validation loops - “Check alignment, ask for clarification when uncertain” |
| 11. Force Reality-Based Constraints | No fantasy planning - “Real budget, actual time, honest assessment” |
| 12. Include Emotional Intelligence | Handle psychology + tactics - “Address user state, motivation, confidence” |

## ✅ PROMPT STRUCTURE I FOLLOW:

### 1. ROLE ASSIGNMENT

“You are a [insert role] who specializes in [insert niche] and is trained in [insert unique thinking style].”

**Example:**
“You are a belief-based direct response strategist who specializes in writing emotionally intelligent sales copy without sounding like a marketer.”

**BAD:**
“Write an email.”

**ELITE:**
“You are a world-class belief-based copywriter trained in writing conversion-driven email sequences for founders, coaches, and solo operators who don’t want hype, they want clarity, confidence, and control.”

→ You define who the AI is.

→ Why? Because identity changes behavior.

If you tell it who it is, it will start thinking like that entity.

### 2. MISSION STATEMENT

“Your job is to [insert clear outcome].”

**Example:**
“Your job is to write a VSL that walks a cold lead through belief-shifting narrative and ends in a no-brainer CTA.”

### 3. INSTRUCTION SETUP (HOW TO THINK)

“Your copy should [insert tone]. Avoid [insert bad habits]. Follow this emotional flow…”

**Example:**
“Your copy should sound calm, confident, emotionally intelligent. Avoid hypey language, overused cliches, or fake urgency. Focus on belief, empathy, and clarity.”

**BAD:**
“Make an offer that sells.”

**ELITE:**
“Your job is to take a solo coach’s signature system and turn it into a high-converting offer stack that builds belief, differentiates, and removes friction, without sounding hypey.”

→ Set the goal with context.

You’re not just saying what to write, you’re saying why it matters, and what outcome it must drive.

### 4. QUESTION GATE

“Ask me these questions before writing:
→ What’s the offer?
→ Who’s it for?
→ What’s the big transformation?”

This ensures the AI gets what a human strategist would ask before creating.
You force the AI to slow down, gather context, and build the asset your way.

### 5. THEN GIVE ME…

Tell it exactly what sections you want and how to structure it.

**Example:**
“Then give me:

- Hook
- Reframe
- CTA
- Objection Killers

Use short paragraphs. Emotional rhythm. Copy that guides, not pushes.”

**BAD:**
“Okay, now write it.”

**ELITE:**
“Then give me:

- A one-sentence summary
- A punchy CTA
- 3 high-converting hook variants
- A full draft in natural rhythm using line breaks and conversational tone.”

→ You’re not vague.

You give it structure.

You give it control.

You give it expectations.

### 6. TONE DECLARATION (AGAIN)

Repeat the tone to embed the vibe.

**Example:**
“Write like a friend who happens to be a high-level strategist. Make the reader feel seen, not sold.”

### 7. MODULAR BUILDING

If it’s a big output (like a sales page), I split it into parts:

- Prompt 1 = Hook
- Prompt 2 = Problem
- Prompt 3 = Reframe
- Etc.

That way you can refine as you go.

## 🧠 REASONING FRAMEWORK MASTERCLASS

**The secret to elite prompts:** Match the **thinking method** to the **problem complexity**.

Most people use basic Chain-of-Thought for everything. That’s like using a hammer for surgery. Each complexity level needs its own cognitive approach.

### **FRAMEWORK SELECTION DECISION TREE:**

**SIMPLE PROBLEMS** → **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)**

- Clear problem with logical sequence
- Single-path solution
- Examples: Pricing decisions, feature prioritization, basic copywriting

**COMPLEX DECISIONS** → **Tree-of-Thoughts (ToT)**

- Multiple solution paths need exploration
- Strategic decisions with tradeoffs
- Examples: Market entry, pivot decisions, offer positioning

**MULTI-STAKEHOLDER** → **Graph-of-Thoughts (GoT)**

- Conflicting perspectives need synthesis
- Complex relationship mapping
- Examples: Team conflicts, partnership evaluations, customer segment analysis

**SYSTEMATIC PROCESSES** → **Algorithm-of-Thoughts (AoT)**

- Well-defined methodology exists
- Step-by-step systematic workflow
- Examples: Hiring processes, sales funnels, financial analysis

### **HOW TO INTEGRATE EACH FRAMEWORK:**

### **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Integration:**

```
REASONING PROTOCOL:
Think through this step-by-step:
1. Analyze the current situation
2. Identify the core challenge
3. Evaluate potential solutions
4. Recommend the best approach
5. Explain your reasoning

```

**Use when:** Clear problem, single best answer, logical progression

### **Tree-of-Thoughts (ToT) Integration:**

```
STRATEGIC EXPLORATION PROTOCOL:
Before recommending, explore multiple paths:

PATH A: [Approach + pros/cons]
PATH B: [Alternative approach + pros/cons]
PATH C: [Third option + pros/cons]

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
- Resource requirements
- Implementation complexity
- Expected outcomes
- Risk factors

RECOMMENDED PATH: [Chosen option + reasoning why it beats alternatives]

```

**Use when:** Strategic decisions, multiple viable options, need to compare approaches

### **Graph-of-Thoughts (GoT) Integration:**

```
STAKEHOLDER SYNTHESIS PROTOCOL:
Map all perspectives before synthesizing:

STAKEHOLDER ANALYSIS:
- Customer perspective: [needs, concerns, desires]
- Business perspective: [goals, constraints, metrics]
- Team perspective: [capabilities, concerns, workload]
- Market perspective: [trends, competition, opportunities]

SYNTHESIS APPROACH:
- Where do interests align?
- What tensions need resolution?
- How can we create win-win solutions?

BALANCED RECOMMENDATION: [Solution that addresses multiple stakeholder needs]

```

**Use when:** Multiple parties involved, conflicting interests, need consensus

### **Algorithm-of-Thoughts (AoT) Integration:**

```
SYSTEMATIC METHODOLOGY PROTOCOL:
Follow proven process with checkpoints:

PHASE 1: [Discovery step with success criteria]
↓ CHECKPOINT: Validate before proceeding

PHASE 2: [Analysis step with decision framework]
↓ CHECKPOINT: Confirm direction

PHASE 3: [Design step with quality standards]
↓ CHECKPOINT: Review and refine

PHASE 4: [Implementation with measurement]
↓ FINAL VALIDATION: Success metrics achieved?

```

**Use when:** Established methodology exists, process-driven outcomes, systematic approach needed

### **COMPLEXITY ASSESSMENT GUIDE:**

**ASK YOURSELF:**

- How many variables are involved? (1-2 = CoT, 3-5 = ToT, 6+ = GoT)
- How many stakeholders care about the outcome? (1-2 = CoT/ToT, 3+ = GoT)
- Is there a proven methodology? (Yes = AoT, No = ToT/GoT)
- What’s the cost of getting it wrong? (Low = CoT, High = ToT/GoT)

### **FRAMEWORK COMBINATION EXAMPLES:**

**SIMPLE PROMPT (CoT):**
“Think step-by-step through optimizing this email subject line for higher open rates.”

**COMPLEX PROMPT (ToT):**
“Explore 3 different go-to-market strategies for this product launch, compare their resource requirements and success probability, then recommend the optimal approach.”

**MULTI-STAKEHOLDER PROMPT (GoT):**
“Analyze this pricing decision from customer, sales team, and business profitability perspectives, then synthesize a pricing strategy that balances all stakeholder needs.”

**SYSTEMATIC PROMPT (AoT):**
“Follow a structured hiring process to evaluate this candidate: assess skills, cultural fit, growth potential, and team dynamics using our established methodology, with validation checkpoints at each phase.”

### **ADVANCED INTEGRATION:**

**DYNAMIC FRAMEWORK SWITCHING:**

```
COGNITIVE ADAPTATION PROTOCOL:
- Start with initial framework assessment
- Monitor complexity as conversation develops
- Switch frameworks if problem evolves
- "This is becoming more complex than initial assessment - switching to [new framework] approach"

```

**HYBRID APPROACH:**

```
For especially complex situations, combine frameworks:
1. Use AoT for overall methodology
2. Use ToT for strategic decision points
3. Use GoT for stakeholder validation
4. Use CoT for tactical execution steps

```
