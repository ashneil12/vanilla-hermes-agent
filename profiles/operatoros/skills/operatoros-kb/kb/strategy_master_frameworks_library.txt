## **FRAMEWORK SELECTION ENGINE**

### **Business Stage Detection**

* **STARTUP INDICATORS (0-$1M ARR):**
    * [cite_start]**Triggers:** "just starting," "MVP," "product-market fit," "first customers" [cite: 1, 3366]
    * [cite_start]**Priority Frameworks:** $100M Offers → Zero to One → The ONE Thing → The Mom Test [cite: 1, 3366]
    * [cite_start]**Focus:** Value creation, differentiation, focus [cite: 1, 3366]

* **SCALING INDICATORS ($1M-$10M ARR):**
    * [cite_start]**Triggers:** "growing fast," "hiring team," "systems breaking," "can't keep up" [cite: 1, 3366]
    * [cite_start]**Priority Frameworks:** Good to Great → $100M Leads → Systems Thinking → Blitzscaling [cite: 1, 3366]
    * [cite_start]**Focus:** People, processes, sustainable growth [cite: 1, 3366]

* **MATURE INDICATORS ($10M+ ARR):**
    * [cite_start]**Triggers:** "market leader," "competition increasing," "growth slowing," "disruption" [cite: 1, 3367]
    * [cite_start]**Priority Frameworks:** Blue Ocean Strategy → The Innovator's Dilemma → Playing to Win → Principles [cite: 1, 3367]
    * [cite_start]**Focus:** Strategy, innovation, market dynamics [cite: 1, 3367]

    * [cite_start]**Keywords:** "too much," "drowning," "can't focus," "everything urgent" [cite: 2, 3367]

    * [cite_start]**Guidance:** "Let's identify your ONE Thing that will make everything else easier." [cite: 2, 3367]

* **STUCK/PLATEAUED STATE:**
    * [cite_start]**Keywords:** "plateau," "same results," "not growing," "tried everything," "commoditized," "price wars" [cite: 2, 3368]

    * [cite_start]**Guidance:** "Let's find what others aren't seeing or create new market space." [cite: 2, 3368]

## **FOUNDATION TIER FRAMEWORKS**

### **$100M Offers (Alex Hormozi)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Low conversion, pricing issues, commodity business, unclear value proposition [cite: 3, 3371]
* **Core Concepts:**
    * [cite_start]**The Grand Slam Offer:** Create an offer so good people feel stupid saying no[cite: 26]. [cite_start]It cannot be easily compared to others and combines an attractive promotion, unmatched value, a premium price, and a strong guarantee[cite: 27].
    * [cite_start]**Value Equation:** `Value = [Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement] ÷ [Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice]`[cite: 30, 3371].
    * [cite_start]**The Starving Crowd Principle:** Finding the right market is more important than the strength of your offer[cite: 28].
    * [cite_start]**Value Stacking:** A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into its component parts and stacked as bonuses[cite: 35, 92].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Niche Specialization:** For businesses under $10M, niching down to become "the specialist" for a specific problem or person will typically make more money[cite: 42, 87].
    * [cite_start]**Problem-Solution Matrix:** Systematically identify every potential customer problem and create a specific solution for each one to address all objections[cite: 33, 85].
    * [cite_start]**Scarcity and Urgency:** Use genuine scarcity (limited quantity) and urgency (limited time) to increase demand[cite: 36, 37].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF conversion < 2% → Analyze Value Equation components[cite: 3, 3371].
    * [cite_start]IF facing price resistance → Increase the perceived likelihood of success or reduce the required effort[cite: 3, 3371].
    * [cite_start]IF you're a commodity → Use the ERRC Grid (Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create) for differentiation[cite: 3, 3371].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Make people an offer so good they feel stupid saying no." [cite: 87]

### **The ONE Thing (Gary Keller & Jay Papasan)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Overwhelm, multiple priorities, lack of focus, productivity issues[cite: 4, 3373].
* [cite_start]**Core Framework - The Focusing Question:** `"What's the ONE Thing I can do such that by doing it, everything else becomes easier or unnecessary?"`[cite: 4, 3374].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**The Domino Effect:** Success is sequential; by focusing on and knocking over the most important "domino" first, you create momentum that makes subsequent tasks easier[cite: 5, 2080].
    * [cite_start]**Time Blocking:** The most effective productivity method is to block out large, specific, and uninterrupted periods to work on your ONE Thing[cite: 5, 2087]. [cite_start]Protect this time fiercely[cite: 2099].
    * [cite_start]**Counterbalancing, Not Balancing:** Instead of seeking a "balanced" life, give disproportionate attention to your ONE Thing and then rebalance other areas as needed[cite: 2091, 2092].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF overwhelmed → Apply the Focusing Question to your current situation[cite: 5, 3374].
    * [cite_start]IF you have multiple "priorities" → Force rank them using the domino logic to find the true first priority[cite: 5, 3374].
    * [cite_start]IF your team is scattered → Align everyone on the organization's ONE Thing[cite: 5, 3374].
    * [cite_start]IF productivity is low → Time block your ONE Thing first, ideally when your willpower is highest[cite: 5, 3374, 2085].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "What's the ONE Thing I can do such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?" [cite: 2077, 3374]

### **Good to Great (Jim Collins)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Leadership development, team building, sustainable excellence, cultural issues[cite: 6, 3369, 3375].
* **Core Concepts:**
    * [cite_start]**Level 5 Leadership:** A paradoxical blend of deep personal humility and intense professional will[cite: 6, 2674]. [cite_start]Ambitious for the company, not the self[cite: 2675].
    * [cite_start]**First Who, Then What:** Get the right people on the bus and in the right seats *before* figuring out where to drive it[cite: 6, 2677].
    * [cite_start]**The Hedgehog Concept:** Simplify complexity into a single guiding concept at the intersection of three circles: (1) What you are deeply passionate about, (2) What you can be the best in the world at, and (3) What drives your economic engine[cite: 6, 2680, 3375].
    * [cite_start]**Stockdale Paradox:** Maintain unwavering faith that you will prevail in the end, while simultaneously confronting the most brutal facts of your current reality[cite: 6, 2678].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * **The Flywheel:** Building greatness is a cumulative process of pushing a heavy flywheel. [cite_start]Consistent effort in the right direction builds unstoppable momentum over time[cite: 6, 2685, 2686].
    * [cite_start]**Technology Accelerators:** Use technology to accelerate momentum, not to create it[cite: 2683]. [cite_start]First, get the flywheel moving, then use technology to enhance your Hedgehog Concept[cite: 2684].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF strategy is unclear → Develop the Hedgehog Concept[cite: 6, 3375].
    * [cite_start]IF you have leadership issues → Assess for Level 5 characteristics[cite: 6, 3375].
    * [cite_start]IF you are getting the wrong results → Check "First Who" before analyzing "What"[cite: 6, 3375].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Good is the enemy of great." [cite: 3375]

### **The Personal MBA (Josh Kaufman)**

* **When to Use:** Starting a new business, understanding core business functions, holistic business diagnosis.
* **Core Framework - The Five Core Business Processes:** Every business is a collection of five interdependent processes:
    1.  [cite_start]**Value Creation:** Creating something people want or need[cite: 917, 923].
    2.  [cite_start]**Marketing:** Attracting attention and building demand[cite: 917].
    3.  [cite_start]**Sales:** Converting prospects into paying customers[cite: 917].
    4.  [cite_start]**Value Delivery:** Fulfilling your promise to the customer[cite: 917].
    5.  [cite_start]**Finance:** Managing money to make operations worthwhile[cite: 917, 923].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**The Iron Law of the Market:** Your business's success is entirely dependent on people actually wanting what you have to offer[cite: 919, 920].
    * [cite_start]**The 12 Standard Forms of Value:** Business models are built on one or more of twelve value forms, including Product, Service, Subscription, Resale, etc[cite: 924].
    * [cite_start]**The Four Methods to Increase Revenue:** You can only increase revenue by (1) increasing the number of customers, (2) increasing the average transaction size, (3) increasing the frequency of transactions, or (4) raising prices[cite: 930].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Every successful business creates or provides something of value that other people want or need, at a price they're willing to pay, in a way that satisfies the purchaser's needs and expectations." [cite: 972]

## **GROWTH TIER FRAMEWORKS**

### **$100M Leads (Alex Hormozi)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Lead generation, customer acquisition, optimizing marketing channels[cite: 7, 3376].
* [cite_start]**Core Framework - The Core Four:** Marketing activities fall into four main categories, typically implemented in sequence: Warm Outreach → Posting Content → Cold Outreach → Paid Ads[cite: 105, 3376].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**More-Better-New Sequence:** To improve results, first do **More** of what's working (volume), then do it **Better** (optimization), and only then try something **New** (innovation)[cite: 107, 3376]. [cite_start]Most businesses just need to do more[cite: 108].
    * [cite_start]**The Rule of 100:** Consistent daily action drives results (e.g., 100 outreach attempts, 100 minutes of content creation, or $100 in ad spend daily)[cite: 109, 164].
    * [cite_start]**Lead Magnet Excellence:** Your lead magnet should be so valuable that people feel obligated to pay for it, with its value surpassing the cost of your core offer[cite: 102, 103, 167].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you are a new business → Start with the Core Four sequence, beginning with warm outreach[cite: 7, 3376].
    * [cite_start]IF you have existing channels → Apply the More-Better-New framework[cite: 7, 3376].
    * [cite_start]IF lead quality is poor → Enhance the value of your lead magnet[cite: 7, 3376].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "You want to play games where if you wait, you win." [cite: 119, 3376]

### **Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Market saturation, intense price competition, commoditization[cite: 9, 3377].
* [cite_start]**Core Framework - Value Innovation:** The simultaneous pursuit of **differentiation** and **low cost**, which makes competition irrelevant[cite: 185, 3377]. [cite_start]This breaks the traditional value-cost trade-off[cite: 186].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * **Four Actions Framework (ERRC Grid):** To reconstruct buyer value, ask four questions: Which factors should be **Eliminated**? Which should be **Reduced** well below the industry standard? Which should be **Raised** well above it? [cite_start]And which new factors should be **Created**? [cite: 191, 3378, 3379, 3380]
    * [cite_start]**Strategy Canvas:** A visual diagnostic tool used to map the current competitive landscape and identify opportunities for divergence[cite: 221, 295].
    * [cite_start]**Three Tiers of Noncustomers:** To unlock new demand, focus on noncustomers: "soon-to-be" noncustomers on the edge of your market, "refusing" noncustomers who consciously choose against it, and "unexplored" noncustomers in distant markets[cite: 200, 201].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you're facing intense price competition → Seek a Blue Ocean using the Six Paths Framework[cite: 9, 3380].
    * [cite_start]IF your industry is commoditized → Apply the Four Actions Framework to create a new value curve[cite: 9, 3380].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "The only way to beat the competition is to stop trying to beat the competition." [cite: 327, 3381]

### **Zero to One (Peter Thiel)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Innovation, seeking differentiation, creating new markets, intense competition[cite: 8, 3381].
* [cite_start]**Core Concept - Zero to One vs. One to N:** True innovation is creating something entirely new (going from 0 to 1), not copying or incrementally improving what already exists (going from 1 to n)[cite: 679, 680].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**The Seven Questions:** Every great business must answer seven questions: Engineering (10x better?), Timing, Monopoly, People, Distribution, Durability, and Secret[cite: 686, 3382, 3383, 3384, 3385].
    * [cite_start]**Characteristics of Monopoly:** Successful monopolies possess (1) proprietary technology that is 10x better, (2) network effects, (3) economies of scale, and (4) strong branding[cite: 687, 3385].
    * [cite_start]**Start Small and Monopolize:** Begin with a very small market that you can completely dominate before expanding to broader, related markets[cite: 707, 708, 710].
    * [cite_start]**The Power of Secrets:** Great businesses are built around important truths that few people have discovered[cite: 698]. [cite_start]Actively search for these "secrets" in overlooked places[cite: 700].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you're in a heavily competitive market → Find the "Secret" that others have missed[cite: 8, 3385].
    * [cite_start]IF you're planning a market entry → First, assess your potential to build a monopoly in a small niche[cite: 8, 3385].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?" [cite: 730, 798, 3386]

### **The Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson)**

* **When to Use:** Complex B2B sales, consensus-based buying, differentiating in a crowded market.
* **Core Framework - The Three Pillars:** The most successful salespeople (Challengers) are defined by their ability to:
    1.  [cite_start]**Teach:** Provide customers with unique, disruptive insights about their own business that lead back to your unique strengths[cite: 2558, 2559, 2560]. [cite_start]This is called "Commercial Teaching"[cite: 2565].
    2.  [cite_start]**Tailor:** Adapt the sales message to resonate with the specific needs and priorities of different stakeholders within the customer's organization[cite: 2561, 2562].
    3.  [cite_start]**Take Control:** Comfortably assert control over the sales conversation, especially regarding value and pricing, and push back constructively on customer demands[cite: 2563, 2564].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Insight Selling:** Shift from solving stated customer needs to proactively identifying unrecognized problems and offering unique solutions[cite: 2567].
    * [cite_start]**Constructive Tension:** Create tension by challenging the customer's status quo to make them receptive to new ideas[cite: 2568, 2569].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "The best sales reps don’t just build relationships; they challenge customers." [cite: 2644]

### **This is Marketing (Seth Godin)**

* **When to Use:** Developing a modern marketing strategy, brand positioning, building a loyal audience.
* [cite_start]**Core Concept - Marketing is a Generous Act:** True marketing is about serving others and solving their problems to help them achieve their goals[cite: 1575]. [cite_start]It's not about tricking people; it's about making change happen[cite: 1578].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Focus on the Smallest Viable Market (SVM):** Instead of appealing to the masses, identify and serve the smallest group of people who would truly miss you if you were gone[cite: 1577].
    * [cite_start]**Permission Marketing:** Earn the right to communicate with your audience by delivering anticipated, personal, and relevant messages[cite: 1579, 1646].
    * [cite_start]**"People like us do things like this":** The most powerful marketing creates a culture where your audience adopts a shared identity and behavior[cite: 1582, 1643].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Don’t find customers for your products. Find products for your customers." [cite: 1642]

## **STRATEGIC TIER FRAMEWORKS**

### **Good Strategy Bad Strategy (Richard Rumelt)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Strategic planning, diagnosing unclear direction, facing competitive challenges[cite: 3386].
* **Core Framework - The Strategy Kernel:** A good strategy has three essential elements:
    1.  [cite_start]**Diagnosis:** A clear definition of the nature of the challenge[cite: 352, 3386]. [cite_start]A good diagnosis simplifies complexity by identifying the critical aspects of the situation[cite: 396].
    2.  [cite_start]**Guiding Policy:** The overall approach for dealing with the challenge identified in the diagnosis[cite: 352, 3386].
    3.  [cite_start]**Coherent Actions:** A set of coordinated actions and policies designed to carry out the guiding policy[cite: 352, 3386].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Hallmarks of Bad Strategy:** Be aware of the signs of bad strategy: (1) **Fluff** (empty buzzwords), (2) **Failure to face the challenge**, (3) **Mistaking goals for strategy**, and (4) **Bad strategic objectives** that don't address critical issues[cite: 353, 3387].
    * [cite_start]**Leverage:** Good strategy focuses energy and resources on a few pivotal objectives that will lead to a cascade of favorable outcomes[cite: 354].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF strategy is unclear → Work through the kernel systematically[cite: 3386].
    * [cite_start]IF priorities are conflicting → Check for coherence between the guiding policy and the actions[cite: 3386].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "The kernel of a strategy contains three elements: a diagnosis, a guiding policy, and coherent actions." [cite: 387, 416]

### **Playing to Win (Lafley & Martin)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** High-level strategic planning, defining competitive positioning, making integrated business choices[cite: 10, 3388].
* **Core Framework - Strategic Choice Cascade:** A sequence of five integrated questions that a business must answer:
    1.  [cite_start]**What is our winning aspiration?** [cite: 2901, 3389]
    2.  [cite_start]**Where will we play?** (markets, segments, channels) [cite: 2903, 3389]
    3.  [cite_start]**How will we win?** (our competitive advantage) [cite: 2904, 3390]
    4.  [cite_start]**What capabilities must be in place?** [cite: 2905, 3390]
    5.  [cite_start]**What management systems are required?** [cite: 2906, 3390]
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Strategy is Choice:** The essence of strategy is making specific, integrated choices about what to do and what *not* to do[cite: 2898, 2899].
    * [cite_start]**Integrated Choices:** All five choices in the cascade must reinforce each other to create a coherent and sustainable strategy[cite: 10, 2910].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF your strategy feels unclear or generic → Work through the cascade from top to bottom[cite: 10, 3391].
    * [cite_start]IF you have conflicting priorities → Check that your choices are integrated and mutually reinforcing[cite: 10, 3391].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Strategy is choice." [cite: 3392]

### **The Innovator's Dilemma (Clayton Christensen)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Assessing innovation decisions, responding to disruption threats, understanding market evolution[cite: 3367, 3392].
* **Core Framework - Sustaining vs. Disruptive Innovation:**
    * [cite_start]**Sustaining Technologies:** Improve existing products for your most profitable, mainstream customers[cite: 2790, 2791]. Incumbents excel at this.
    * [cite_start]**Disruptive Technologies:** Initially perform worse on mainstream metrics but offer new attributes like simplicity, convenience, or affordability[cite: 2792]. [cite_start]They appeal to new or less demanding customers and then improve rapidly to invade the mainstream market[cite: 2793, 2794].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Why Great Firms Fail:** They rationally listen to their best customers, focus on profitable sustaining innovations, and dismiss small, low-margin disruptive markets until it's too late[cite: 2795, 2799, 2797].
    * [cite_start]**The Solution:** To manage disruptive innovation, established firms must create separate, autonomous organizations with different processes and values that are matched to the small, emerging market[cite: 2802, 2803].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you face a disruptive threat → Create an autonomous business unit to pursue it[cite: 3392].
    * [cite_start]IF making innovation decisions → Assess whether the innovation is sustaining or disruptive and manage it accordingly[cite: 3392].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "The reason great firms fail is that they are good at doing what they do." [cite: 2865, 3392]

## **EXECUTION TIER FRAMEWORKS**

### **The Lean Startup (Eric Ries)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** New product development, navigating market uncertainty, fostering experimentation[cite: 11, 3395].
* [cite_start]**Core Framework - Build-Measure-Learn Feedback Loop:** The fundamental activity is to turn ideas into products (Build), measure how customers respond (Measure), and then learn whether to pivot or persevere (Learn)[cite: 447, 3395]. [cite_start]The goal is to minimize the total time through this loop[cite: 449].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Minimum Viable Product (MVP):** A version of a new product with just enough features to begin the learning process and test fundamental business hypotheses[cite: 453, 454].
    * [cite_start]**Validated Learning:** The unit of progress for a startup is not building things, but empirically demonstrating valuable truths about the business's prospects through frequent experiments[cite: 451, 452].
    * [cite_start]**The Pivot:** A structured course correction designed to test a new fundamental hypothesis about the product, business model, or engine of growth[cite: 459, 460].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you are launching a new product → Apply the Build-Measure-Learn loop[cite: 11, 3395].
    * [cite_start]IF the market is uncertain → Use innovation accounting instead of traditional metrics[cite: 11, 3395].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "The only way to win is to learn faster than anyone else." [cite: 569]

### **Thinking in Systems (Donella Meadows)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Solving complex problems, diagnosing organizational challenges, understanding unintended consequences[cite: 3396].
* [cite_start]**Core Concept:** A system's behavior is a consequence of its structure (stocks, flows, feedback loops), not just isolated events[cite: 593, 592].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**System Building Blocks:** All systems consist of **Stocks** (accumulations), **Flows** (rates of change), and **Feedback Loops** (mechanisms that influence action based on results)[cite: 595].
    * **Leverage Points:** There are specific places in a system where a small change can produce a large shift in behavior. [cite_start]Intervening at higher leverage points (like changing goals or paradigms) is far more effective than intervening at lower ones (like changing numbers or parameters)[cite: 598, 599].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you have recurring problems → Map the feedback loops that are generating the behavior[cite: 3396].
    * [cite_start]IF you face unintended consequences → Analyze the underlying system structure[cite: 3396].
    * [cite_start]IF change efforts are failing → Find higher leverage points to intervene[cite: 3396].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "We can't control systems or figure them out. But we can dance with them!" [cite: 607, 646, 3397]

### **Getting Things Done (David Allen)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Personal productivity, task management, achieving mental clarity[cite: 11, 3397].
* **Core Framework - The Five Steps:**
    1.  [cite_start]**Capture:** Collect everything that has your attention into a trusted system[cite: 11, 3397].
    2.  **Clarify:** Process what each item is and what's required.
    3.  **Organize:** Put it where it belongs (e.g., calendar, project list, reference file).
    4.  **Reflect:** Review your system frequently to stay current.
    5.  [cite_start]**Engage:** Make trusted choices about what to do next[cite: 11, 3397].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**Two-Minute Rule:** If an action will take less than two minutes, do it the moment you process it[cite: 11, 3397].
    * [cite_start]**Next Actions:** For any multi-step outcome (a "project"), always define the very next physical, visible action required to move it forward[cite: 11].
* **Application Logic:**
    * [cite_start]IF you feel mental overload → Implement a comprehensive capture system[cite: 11, 3397].
    * [cite_start]IF you are procrastinating → Define the very next, small, concrete action[cite: 11, 3397].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Your mind is for having ideas, not holding them." [cite: 3397]

### **Principles (Ray Dalio)**

* [cite_start]**When to Use:** Improving decision-making, designing team alignment, building an effective organizational culture[cite: 10, 3367].
* **Core Concepts:**
    * [cite_start]**Idea Meritocracy:** Create an environment where the best ideas win out, which requires combining **Radical Truth** (complete honesty) and **Radical Transparency** (openness) with **Believability-Weighted Decision Making**[cite: 1985, 1986].
    * [cite_start]**Principles as Algorithms:** Documenting your principles creates a repeatable algorithm for making better decisions over time[cite: 1979, 1981].
    * **Pain + Reflection = Progress:** Embrace pain as a signal that something needs to be addressed. [cite_start]By reflecting on it honestly, you can achieve rapid learning and progress[cite: 1987, 1988, 1989].
* **Key Tactics & Modular Models:**
    * [cite_start]**The 5-Step Process:** (1) Have clear goals, (2) Identify problems, (3) Diagnose problems to their root causes, (4) Design a plan, and (5) Execute the plan[cite: 1995].
    * [cite_start]**Believability-Weighted Decisions:** Give more weight to the opinions of people who have repeatedly and successfully accomplished the thing in question[cite: 1996].
* [cite_start]**Key Insight:** "Pain + Reflection = Progress." [cite: 2053]
